Spiritual Progression: Agency, Stewardship, and Growth

Reflection on Agency

This week I reflected deeply on the principle of agency and how it shapes both my spiritual and professional development. Doctrine and Covenants 58:26–28 teaches that we are not meant to be acted upon but to act with intention, diligence, and faith. Every meaningful step I have taken academically, professionally, and spiritually has come from choosing to engage, to learn, and to grow.

Personal Story: How Agency Shaped My Journey

One of the most defining moments in my life happened long before I ever wrote a line of code. It was the day I decided to leave home and build a new life in the UK. I didn’t have a perfect plan, and I didn’t know exactly how things would unfold. But I felt a quiet, steady impression that growth required movement, that staying where I was would keep me safe, but not help me become who I needed to be.

Those early months were hard. I was navigating a new culture, new responsibilities, and new expectations. There were days when I questioned whether I had made the right choice. But every time I prayed or reflected, I felt the same message: Act. Move. Trust.

Today, when I face challenges in my IT work or personal life, I draw strength from that season. It reminds me that God magnifies small steps, and that progress often begins with a single, imperfect decision to try.

Stewardship Through Work

My spiritual progression is tied closely to my work. I see my technical abilities as a form of stewardship. The Lord has entrusted me with the capacity to understand systems, data, infrastructure, and the ethical responsibilities that accompany them. When I design secure systems, communicate clearly, or solve complex problems, I feel aligned with my spiritual values.

Talks and Scriptures Guiding My Journey

  • Come What May, and Love It — Elder Joseph B. Wirthlin
  • Be Not Afraid, Only Believe — Elder Jeffrey R. Holland
  • Doctrine and Covenants 58:26–28 — Acting in faith and diligence

These teachings remind me that faith and effort work together. They guide how I approach challenges in both my personal life and my IT career.

Spiritual Identity and Daily Work

My work becomes more than a task, it becomes a way to honour God, protect His children, and contribute to something meaningful. I want to build systems that uplift, protect, and serve and not just function.

🙏 Walking in purpose, guided by faith and discipleship ✨
