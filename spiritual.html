Spiritual Progression Portfolio

Principles, experiences, and reflections that guide my discipleship and personal growth.

My Spiritual Progression

My spiritual development has shaped the way I learn, work, and serve. As I grow academically and professionally, I rely on spiritual principles that help me stay grounded, focused, and aligned with God’s purpose for my life.

Guiding Principle 1: Trust in God’s Timing and Direction

Trusting in God’s timing has helped me navigate major transitions in my life — from engineering, to electrical work, to renewable energy, and now into Information Technology.

Scriptures and Insights

  • Isaiah 40:31 — Strength comes as I wait upon the Lord.
  • Alma 37:37 — Counsel with the Lord in all my doings.
  • Personal experiences where unexpected opportunities opened after prayer.

Personal Reflection

Through prayer and reflection, I realized that God was not delaying me — He was preparing me. Trusting His timing has helped me stay calm, focused, and confident in the direction I’m moving.

Guiding Principle 2: Diligence and Consistent Effort

My engineering background taught me that progress comes through steady, disciplined work. Spiritually, the same principle applies.

Scriptures and Insights

  • Doctrine and Covenants 10:4 — Be diligent and do not run faster than you have strength.
  • 2 Nephi 31:20 — Press forward with steadfastness in Christ.
  • Lessons from engineering projects requiring patience and precision.

Personal Reflection

Whether troubleshooting electrical faults or learning cloud computing, I’ve seen that diligence builds confidence. Spiritually, consistent effort strengthens my relationship with God.

Guiding Principle 3: Service as a Form of Leadership

I believe leadership is rooted in service — lifting others, sharing knowledge, and helping people succeed.

Scriptures and Insights

  • Mosiah 2:17 — When you serve others, you serve God.
  • Experiences helping classmates and coworkers understand technical concepts.
  • Church messages about Christlike leadership.

Personal Reflection

As I grow in IT, I want my skills to bless others — solving problems, supporting teams, and contributing to meaningful work.

Additional Spiritual Influences

  • Gratitude and Humility: Recognizing God’s hand in my progress keeps me grounded.
  • Prayer and Revelation: Prayer has guided major decisions in my education and career.
  • Resilience: Challenges in engineering, migration, and education have strengthened my faith.

My Spiritual Growth Journey

Spiritual growth and professional growth work together. The qualities that help me become a better disciple — trust, diligence, service, humility, and resilience — also help me become a stronger engineer, a better IT professional, and a more grounded person.
