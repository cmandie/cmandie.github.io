Projects & Ethical Dilemma Analysis

Project 1: IT Troubleshooting & Systems Support

Technical Achievement: I diagnosed and resolved hardware and software issues for multiple computers, improving system performance and user experience. This included identifying faulty components, updating software, and optimising system settings.

Spiritual Integration: This project strengthened my patience and Christlike communication. I learned to explain technical issues calmly and respectfully, focusing on helping users feel supported rather than overwhelmed. It reminded me that service and compassion can be expressed through technical skills.

Project 2: Electrical & Power Distribution Work

Technical Achievement: I worked on overhead and underground powerline maintenance, applying safety procedures, troubleshooting techniques, and system reliability practices. I handled fault isolation and emergency response, contributing to stable power delivery.

Spiritual Integration: This work taught me discipline, accountability, and humility. In high‑risk environments, integrity is non‑negotiable. I learned to rely on the Lord for calmness and clarity, especially when making decisions that affected the safety of others.

Project 3: Professional Writing & Ethical Analysis (IT 497)

Technical Achievement: I developed business writing, transactional writing, and technical documentation skills. I also analysed an ethical dilemma involving misuse of administrative privileges, evaluating the consequences and appropriate responses.

Spiritual Integration: This project helped me see how discipleship influences professional ethics. Integrity, honesty, and accountability are spiritual principles that directly shape ethical behaviour in IT environments. I learned that disciple‑leadership means choosing what is right even when it is difficult.

Ethical Dilemma Summary

The ethical dilemma involved an employee accessing restricted files without authorization. This violated company policy and professional standards. The appropriate response included revoking access, documenting the incident, and issuing disciplinary action. Through this analysis, I learned that ethical behaviour in IT requires integrity, transparency, and accountability — principles that align with my spiritual commitment to honesty and discipleship.
