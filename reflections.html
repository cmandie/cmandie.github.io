Reflections

Insights from my spiritual, academic, and professional journey.

Reflection 1: Beginning My Portfolio Journey

When I began building this portfolio, I viewed it as a simple academic requirement. Over time, it became a mirror of my growth — academically, spiritually, and professionally. Reviewing my engineering background, IT projects, and personal experiences helped me recognize how each step has shaped my identity and future goals.

This process taught me to evaluate my strengths, acknowledge areas for improvement, and appreciate the progress I often overlook in daily life.

Reflection 2: Faith and Professional Growth

My journey has shown me that faith and professional development are deeply connected. The same principles that guide my spiritual life — trust, integrity, humility, and service — influence how I approach technology, teamwork, and problem‑solving.

Faith gives me direction. Integrity shapes my decisions. Service reminds me that technology exists to bless people, not just systems.

Reflection 3: Agency and Personal Responsibility

I have learned that growth requires initiative. Whether studying cloud computing, troubleshooting systems, or improving my engineering knowledge, progress comes when I take responsibility for my learning.

Agency means acting with purpose — not waiting for opportunities, but preparing for them. This mindset has helped me stay focused and proactive in my education and career preparation.

Reflection 4: Ethical Decision‑Making in Technology

Working with technology means working with trust. Whether handling data, configuring systems, or supporting users, ethical behavior is essential.

I have learned that technical skill alone is not enough. A trustworthy IT professional must protect privacy, respect boundaries, and act with integrity.

Ethical Dilemma Analysis

Issue: Misuse of administrator privileges and unauthorized access to confidential information.

Ethical Principles Involved

  • Integrity
  • Accountability
  • Privacy
  • Professional Responsibility
  • Honesty

Better Approach

  • Role‑based access controls
  • Audit logs and monitoring
  • Ethics and security training
  • Clear accountability structures

This experience taught me that ethical values and technical controls must work together to protect systems and people.

Reflection on Temple and Personal Reflection Experience

During my personal reflection time, I focused on my educational journey, engineering background, and transition into IT. I felt gratitude for the opportunities that have shaped me and the guidance I have received through faith.

This experience reminded me that true success is not only about skills — it is about character, purpose, and service.

Looking Forward

As I continue my studies and prepare for a career in Information Technology, I want to remain committed to lifelong learning, ethical leadership, and service. My goal is to become a skilled IT professional who uplifts others through integrity, faith, and responsible use of technology.
