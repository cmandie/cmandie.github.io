Week 5 – Integration, Reflection, and Design Enhancement

Spiritual Alignment • Professional Growth • Ethical Development

Introduction

When I refined my professional growth portfolio, I was aware that my development is not only academic or professional but also spiritual. My journey in electrical engineering, renewable energy, and IT has taught me the value of precision, safety, and ethical responsibility. My spiritual journey teaches me to cultivate Christlike attributes, recognise personal warning signs, and realign with the Saviour's example.

1. Integrating Christlike Attributes into My Life and Work

Humility

In engineering and IT, humility is essential. No matter how much experience I have—from powerline maintenance to system administration—there is always more to learn. Humility allows me to accept correction, seek mentorship, and acknowledge when I need help. Spiritually, humility keeps me grounded in the Saviour's grace and reminds me that progress is a gift.

Patience

Patience is a stabilising force in both technical work and personal discipleship. Whether troubleshooting a complex electrical fault or navigating life transitions, patience helps me avoid rushed decisions and maintain clarity. It also helps me recognise that spiritual growth is gradual and requires consistent effort.

Integrity

Integrity is foundational in engineering and IT, where safety, accuracy, and trust are non‑negotiable. Christlike integrity means being honest even when no one is watching, reporting issues truthfully, and upholding ethical standards. It also means aligning my private life with my public commitments.

Compassion

Compassion helps me to see people beyond tasks and deadlines.

2. Recognising Personal Warning Signs

  • Rushing through tasks instead of being deliberate
  • Becoming overly self‑critical
  • Avoiding prayer or scripture study when stressed
  • Withdrawing from others instead of seeking connection
  • Feeling irritated or impatient over small things

3. Strategies for Realignment

  • Daily spiritual prayer and scripture study
  • Weekly self‑assessment of emotional, spiritual, and professional well‑being
  • Seeking feedback from peers, mentors, and instructors
  • Practising mindful patience during stressful tasks
  • Reconnecting with my purpose: to serve, uplift, and contribute meaningfully

4. Portfolio Integration

My professional achievements should reflect integrity, diligence, and growth. My spiritual reflections should demonstrate humility, gratitude, and discipleship. My design choices must communicate clarity, respect, and professionalism. This document will guide the final polishing of my portfolio, ensuring it is cohesive, authentic, and aligned with my values.

Usability Testing Report & Ethical Dilemma Reflection

Usability Testing Overview

I conducted usability testing with three individuals who represent typical users of my portfolio: a classmate, a family member, and a colleague in the technical field. Each person interacted with the portfolio on different devices (laptop, tablet, and mobile) to identify navigation, readability, and design issues.

Key Findings

  • Some pages required too many clicks to reach important content
  • Long paragraphs needed better spacing and headings
  • Images and text blocks shifted awkwardly on mobile
  • Users wanted more visuals and interactive elements

Enhancements Implemented

Navigation Improvements

  • Added a fixed top navigation bar
  • Simplified menu categories

Design & Visual Enhancements

  • Introduced consistent font hierarchy
  • Added professional icons for sections
  • Improved spacing and alignment for readability

Responsiveness

  • Adjusted image scaling
  • Ensured buttons and links resize properly on mobile

Ethical Dilemma Reflection

The Dilemma

In my line of work, we face pressure to deliver results quickly. The dilemma I chose is about an engineer who discovers a potential safety risk in an electrical installation, but his supervisors encouraged him to mark the work as completed to meet a deadline. The engineer must choose between professional integrity and organisational pressure.

My View & How I Would Respond

  • Document the issue clearly
  • Communicate the risk to management
  • Refuse to sign off until the problem is corrected
  • Propose solutions or alternative timelines
  • Escalate the issue if necessary

How This Exercise Helped Me Professionally

Ethical decisions define professional credibility. Safety and honesty protect both people and organisations.
