Week 6 – Finalization & Reflection

Bringing Together Spiritual and Professional Growth

This week focuses on completing your portfolios, reflecting on your journey, and preparing for presentation.

Reflective Essay: Portfolio Alignment and Goal Evolution

As I reflect on the development of my Spiritual Progression Portfolio and Professional Growth Portfolio, I can see how both have become more than academic assignments—they have become a meaningful record of who I am becoming. When I began this course, my goals were clear but somewhat narrow: strengthen my writing, refine my professional identity, and articulate my spiritual foundations. Over time, these goals expanded and deepened as I recognized how interconnected my spiritual and professional growth truly are.

At first, I approached the portfolio with a focus on organization and presentation. However, as the weeks progressed, I found myself reflecting more intentionally on the principles that guide my life—faith, integrity, accountability, service, and continuous improvement. These principles became the thread that tied every section of my portfolio together. The more I wrote, the more I realized that my spiritual convictions are not separate from my professional ambitions; they shape the way I learn, lead, and serve. This alignment became clearer as I revised earlier sections, added personal stories, and integrated feedback from my peers.

My goals also evolved as I gained a deeper understanding of my strengths and areas for growth. Initially, I focused primarily on technical skills and career preparation. By the end of the course, I recognized the importance of becoming a disciple‑leader—someone who uses technical ability, ethical judgment, and spiritual grounding to uplift others. This shift broadened my vision of what success looks like. It is no longer just about securing a role in IT; it is about becoming a person who leads with compassion, clarity, and purpose.

The peer review process played a significant role in this evolution. The feedback I received encouraged me to strengthen my narrative, add more personal examples, and refine the structure of my portfolios. Implementing this feedback helped me see my work from a new perspective and pushed me to articulate my goals more clearly. It also reminded me that growth is collaborative—something I hope to carry into my future career.

Overall, my portfolios now reflect a more mature and integrated understanding of my spiritual and professional identity. They show where I have been, what I have learned, and who I am striving to become. Moving forward, I plan to continue updating them as I grow in discipleship, technical skill, and leadership. This course has helped me see that my development is ongoing, intentional, and deeply connected to my faith in Jesus Christ.

Thank you for reviewing my Week 6 reflection.
